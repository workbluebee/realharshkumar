About me

Meant to drift.
Forced to anchor.

Hey — you're here. Which means you either Googled me, someone sent you this link, or you clicked something by accident. All three are valid ways to arrive.

Who Harsh Kumar. Social strategist, founder, and the kind of person who reverse-engineers why ads make people cry.
What I run Bluebee Digital — a social media growth agency built on systems and an embarrassing amount of A/B testing. When I'm not doing that, I'm writing on Medium or spiralling on X about brand strategy.
Where Jamshedpur, India. Yes, the Tata city. Yes, the irony of writing about Tata Salt is fully intentional.
Obsessed with Systems. Seriously. The reason brands scale, the reason content goes viral, the reason some campaigns last 30 years — it's always a system underneath. I can't look at a product without asking how it works.
Off the clock Playing with my dog. Reading packaging labels at grocery stores (this counts as research). Occasionally sleeping.
If you're a brand wondering if I can help — probably yes, let's figure it out.
If you're a fellow marketer just reading — welcome, stay a while.
If you're my mom — hi, yes I'm fine, no I won't get a government job.

What I do

How I actually
spend my time.

01
Social Growth Strategy
Instagram and YouTube aren't just apps — they're systems with their own logic. I'm slightly obsessed with decoding that logic and making it work for you instead of against you.
02
Content Automation
Through Bluebee Digital, I build frameworks that keep brands consistent and visible. The goal: your content machine runs without you having to be online at 11pm every single night.
03
Brand Marketing Analysis
I write case studies on why brands stick — from Tata Salt to Zara. If I find something interesting I genuinely cannot not write about it. It's a problem.
04
Platform Presence
Active on X, LinkedIn, Instagram, and Medium. I help you find what to say, where to say it, and how to say it without sounding like a press release nobody asked for.
05
Long-Form Writing
Case studies that read like stories. Marketing explained the way I'd explain it at a chai stall — no jargon, no LinkedIn fluff, just the actual interesting bit.
06
Founder Storytelling
Your story is the most underused thing you have. I help you find the thread that runs through everything you're building — and actually tell it in a way people remember.
